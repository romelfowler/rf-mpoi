A step-by-step guide for creating an pay-per-click marketing campaign for your business

Pay-per-click (PPC) advertising, also known as cost-per-click (CPC) advertising, describes the process of increasing your online presence through the use of paid advertisements on search engines, specifically the Google search engine. When one enters a query into the Google search bar, the search engine results page will show off about 10 different results as set by default settings. The top one to four results will be for business owners who paid for their website to appear near the top position of the result page based on keywords that the user placed into the search box. They will be marked with an ad symbol or surrounded by a box to indicate these websites paid to pair for that specific query. The rest of the results will be organic results determined by Google’s search algorithms that found the most relevant websites to the query placed into the search box. PPC advertisements can be a double-edged sword. On one hand, they can increase exposure of your business to potential consumers who are likely to observe your website and become an actual customer. On the other hand, since PPC advertisements cost your business money every time someone clicks on the link to your website, you can be overwhelmed by users who won’t end up becoming a customer. H ere are some tips and steps to starting your astroturf marketing campaign:

Step 1: Create your own Google Adwords account. This is the platform where you will be running your pay-per-click advertising campaign on Google.

Step 2: Create your own Google analytics account. Here you will be able to observe the statistics of your website, including which of your webpages gain the most attention, the path that viewers use when browsing through the site, the total webpage views, and many more other features. You can fine tune your PPC advertising campaign to what works for your website.

Step 3: Determine the venue or venues where you will be sending your Determine the keywords you will be using for your PPC campaign. Use keywords that are most likely to bring in viewers who are most likely to purchase from your website. This is crucial since you are paying for every time a user clicks on your PPC advertisement.

Step 4: Wait a few weeks to a few months to determine the efficacy of each keyword you’re using to advertise your website.

Step 5: Within a few months, update your keywords to the most relevant keyword of the time. About every six months, Google algorithms on Google Adwords may alter which keywords will be displayed more often.

Step 6: Repeat steps 3 through 5 every few months. It is important to consistently update your ads because your keyword may not be as popular as another keyword at a later time of the year.

Step 7: Take a look at the increase in viewership of your website and reap the benefits of more sales.

Overview

There are numerous ways to market your business in the online world. Pay-per-click advertising simply utilizes the most common and basic way to search for information online, the search engine, specifically the Google search engine. This method of advertising takes advantage of specific keywords in order to better market website. Although this method may allow a website to reach out to a larger number of users, there are drawbacks to the pay-per-click style of advertising. Marketing in this manner costs hundreds, if not thousands of dollars, and in the end, there may still be little to no difference in how many users actually go through and purchase a product or service from your website. Pay-per-click advertising increase the number of people who visit your website, but those people will not necessarily become paying customers and therefore you simply lose money with each person who visits your website through a paid Google ad. Heavily research the keywords you’ll be using to advertise your business or you may end up not gaining a reasonable return for your advertising investment.

Tips

-Choosing the right keywords that users type into search engines are the most important part to any PPC advertising campaign. -The most competitive keyword may not always be the best keyword to use for your campaign. Highly competitive keywords are costly. -Consider choosing keywords that people use when they are most likely to purchase a product or service.
