A short article on understanding what makes your website stand out amongst the millions of other sites on the Internet.

In today’s business world, it takes more than just an established presence in the real world to promote your brand. The online space is where a large majority of the younger generation of individuals and an increasing number of the older generation are going to to find their information. When a user needs to buy an item or search for ideas, one of the most common places they’ll use to find that information is on a search engine, like Google, Yahoo, or Bing. These search engines are similar to the phone directories of the past, except for a very significant feature: your website will appear in a user’s top results page based on how optimized your web page is for that given keyword. In this article, we’ll be talking about Google specifically, since it is the largest and most commonly used search engine. For example, a user types in the keywords “party supplies” and the top ten results beneath the advertised results, indicated by an “ad” symbol, will be for sites that Google algorithms have determined are the most the relevant to you. Based on how those websites have structured their webpages to include the keywords “party supplies,” Google will rank them in order of relevance on the top results page.

In order to optimize your web pages to appear in the results page for these specific keywords, you first need to consider the keywords you’ll be using for each web page. Each web page you have should be optimized for a set of keywords or a single keyword. Any more and this will hurt your chances of Google algorithms placing your website in the top search results.

Ex. Dedicate one web page to “bike tires” and another page to “bike seats”. The more web pages you have dedicated to a single keyword or set of keywords, the more chances you give your website of appearing on a Google search results for a given keyword.

Another idea to keep in mind is that Google algorithms pay little to no mind about the images you have on your website. The algorithms focus only on the keyword content of your site’s pages, not the images. This means that the flashiest or most beautiful website will typically be superseded by websites with dedicated web pages that cater to specific keywords. Images with text in the image are also not easily identified by Google’s search algorithms and therefore will typically not be considered when a user types in a query. The idea to remember is that in order to make your website stand out amongst other websites in search engines, is to make dedicated webpages for specific keywords, rather than focus primarily on the look of the site. The aesthetics of your site will not matter if no one other than you and your colleagues actually see the page.

Avoid the pitfall of making your website discreditable in the eyes of the Google search engine. This means ensuring that when your web pages include certain keywords, the body of the site primarily relates to those keywords. A webpage with the keywords “bike supplies” should not be focusing on “craft ideas.” This makes your website an untrustworthy source of information for Google, and will thusly be buried amongst the hundreds of other search results. Gain online credibility by encouraging other websites to link to your own site. When other websites link to your website, this tells Google that your site is a credible and relevant source for certain keywords. These sites can be any credible website, include social media sites like Facebook and Twitter.

In order to get a better understanding on the mechanics behind how Google search engines work and better optimize your website, take some time to learn some of the different types of Google algorithms. -RankBrain Algorithm: this is the most commonly used algorithm that is based on a single keyword or set of keywords -Panda Algorithm: a series of algorithms that determines the results page based on how much content is on a webpage and if the content is duplicated on other portions of the website -Penguin Algorithm: an algorithm that penalizes websites for over-optimization: this happens when a developer places the same keyword too many times throughout a page. -And many more...

Overview

A website is very similar to a brick-and-mortar business, but you need to know the right tools and techniques that can make your website work for you. In the same way that people of the past placed ads into phonebooks and directories to promote their business, people of today optimize their website to appear in online directories like Google. Modify your website to include concepts and keywords that Google will determine as relevant for specific keywords. When people search for your ideas and items related to your business, make sure that your website comes up as one of the top results for that query.
